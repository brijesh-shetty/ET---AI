# Demo script — PS2 Energy Supply Chain Resilience

Target runtime: 5 minutes. Single presenter at the keyboard. Second presenter narrates from this script.

The platform covers India's full strategic import basket — crude, LNG, coking coal, critical minerals, solar PV, uranium — not just oil. Most teams will scope PS2 to crude only; multi-commodity breadth is our differentiation.

---

## 0:00 - 0:30 — Opening hook

**On screen:** Title slide. Single line: "India's energy supply chain is data-rich, intelligence-poor."

**Narrator:**

> In January 2025 at Vizag Steel Plant, eight workers died in a coke oven explosion. The gas pressure sensors had been reading abnormally for hours. The data was there. The intelligence layer that would have connected those readings to an evacuation decision was not. The same pattern repeats at national scale — across every strategic import India depends on. India brings in 88% of its crude, 50% of its gas as LNG, 85% of its coking coal, 90% of its rare earths, and 80% of its solar panels. Five separate supply chains, mostly transiting four maritime chokepoints, with no unified intelligence layer connecting geopolitical signals to procurement decisions. We built that layer.

---

## 0:30 - 1:30 — Live dashboard

**On screen:** Open `http://localhost:5173/`. Dashboard loads. Point to the four corridor risk metric cards across the top: Hormuz, Bab el-Mandeb, Malacca, China route.

**Narrator:**

> Top of screen: live composite risk scores for the four maritime corridors India's imports transit. Hormuz amber at 62 today — driven by US-Iran tension headlines from GDELT in the last 24 hours, AIS density anomalies near the strait, and a 4% Brent move overnight.

**Action:** Hover over the Hormuz card to surface the top three drivers. Then point to the narrated feed on the lower left.

> The narrated feed is generated by Claude over the live GDELT event stream. Every 15 minutes the system summarises the top three signals driving each corridor's score, in plain analyst language. Below that — the daily executive brief, regenerated each morning, with a market snapshot and recommended actions for India's procurement directors and policymakers.

## 1:30 - 2:30 — Digital twin map

**On screen:** Click the "Digital Twin" link in the sidebar.

**Narrator:**

> The supply chain digital twin. Each colored dot is a maritime corridor. Red, orange, amber, green based on current composite risk. Today, Hormuz is on watch.

**Action:** Click the Hormuz dot. It navigates to the Hormuz scenario detail page.

> One click triggers our pre-configured Hormuz partial closure scenario.

---

## 2:30 - 3:30 — Scenario cascade

**On screen:** Scenario Run page for Hormuz closure. The four impact bars at the top populate: Brent uplift, SPR runway lost, GDP bps, Inflation bps. The cascade timeline chart renders Brent, SPR daily draw, and Cape rerouting share over time.

**Narrator:**

> The scenario engine models 50% volume reduction through Hormuz over 14 days. Brent moves up by roughly 30%. India's strategic petroleum reserves — already only 9.5 days of cover — drop to 5.1 days by week two. GDP takes a 45 basis point hit, inflation passes through at about 35 basis points. The chart on the right shows the day-by-day cascade. Crucially, LNG out of Qatar also goes through Hormuz, so this isn't just an oil shock — the JKM LNG price moves about 18% on the same event.

**Action:** Scroll down to the analyst narrative panel and the sourcing recommendation table.

> Below the chart: the analyst narrative, generated by Claude Opus from the scenario parameters. And underneath that, the sourcing intelligence — top ranked alternatives by country, with current risk scores, lead times, and sanctions checks. Note the explicit caveat: we rank alternatives without claiming refinery chemistry validation. Honesty about scope beats overclaiming.

## 3:30 - 4:15 — Multi-commodity hop

**On screen:** Use the sidebar to go back to Dashboard, then click the "Australian coking coal" scenario card. Then click the "China rare earth export curbs" card.

**Narrator:**

> The same engine handles non-oil supply chains. Australian coking coal disruption — say a Queensland cyclone — drives coking coal prices up 25% and compresses Indian steel-mill margins. We track this because India imports 70% of its coking coal from Queensland, mostly through Paradip and Vizag.

**Action:** Click into the China rare earth scenario.

> China rare earth export curbs are a different commodity, different corridor, same intelligence layer. NdFeB magnet supply contracts hit India's EV and defence sectors. One platform, multiple supply chains.

---

## 4:15 - 4:45 — SPR optimisation

**On screen:** Click "Strategic Reserves" in the sidebar. Adjust the target-cover-days input to 12, horizon to 60, then click "Solve plan".

**Narrator:**

> The strategic reserve optimiser is a linear program. Decision variables are daily drawdown and replenishment across our three SPR sites — Visakhapatnam, Mangalore, Padur. Objective: minimise the price-impact integral over the planning horizon. Subject to reserve, injection-rate, and consumption constraints.

**Action:** Watch the SPR chart render. The blue area shows the optimised drawdown; the orange dashed line is the flat-baseline reference.

> The blue area is the LP solution. The orange dashed line is the baseline — constant daily drawdown, no optimisation. The LP front-loads modestly to stabilise prices in week one, then tapers and overlaps with the replenishment window. McKinsey's analysis of past energy shocks found economies without an integrated response intelligence layer took 47 days longer on average to stabilise supply. The point of this LP is to close that gap.

The architecture is configuration-driven — adding a new corridor or commodity is a YAML edit. Production roadmap: live API integration is wired but flag-gated, then a domain mentor partnership for refinery chemistry on the procurement module, and an NPCIL pilot for uranium scenarios. One intelligence layer for India's full strategic import basket. Thank you.

## Backup demo paths if a beat fails

- If the live ingest call hangs: every endpoint has a fixture fallback driven by `ALLOW_LIVE_INGEST=false` in `.env`. Restart the backend with that flag and re-demo from fixtures.
- If the map renders without dots: confirm the `getTwinState` call returned data; check the network tab for `/api/twin/state`. The page will show "Loading..." if no data has arrived.
- If a scenario panel does not populate the cascade chart: the LLM narrative will still appear from `llm_responses.json` fixtures.
- If the SPR LP solve is slow: PuLP+CBC takes ~1 second on the default horizon. Pre-cache by clicking "Solve plan" once before the demo starts.

## Presenter notes

- Keep eye contact with judges during the dashboard beat; let them absorb the four corridor scores.
- For the scenario cascade beat, pause for two seconds after the impact bars render — the visceral "SPR drops from 9.5 days to 5.1 days" line lands best when it's not rushed.
- The honesty note about refinery chemistry is deliberate. Say it confidently — it differentiates us from teams who'll wave their hands.
- Close with one sentence at most. Time discipline matters more than a perfect closing line.
